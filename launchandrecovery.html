Planning

The company plans the mission, including the launch window, the flight path, and the activities that will take place during the mission. The company performs final checks on the spacecraft and all equipment, and makes any necessary adjustments before the launch.

Launch

Space Horizon's tourism rocket launch involves fueling the rocket with liquid oxygen and rocket-grade kerosene, conducting pre-launch checks, and igniting the engines for liftoff. The rocket then rises into the atmosphere, accelerating as it goes, before separating from its first stage and continuing its journey into space.

Journey

It could be a suborbital flight, reaching an altitude where the passengers can experience a few minutes of weightlessness before returning to Earth. Or it could be an orbital flight, reaching a specific altitude and velocity to orbit the Earth, allowing passengers to see the planet from above and experience a longer period of weightlessness. The journey and destination are carefully planned and executed to ensure the safety and comfort of the passengers.

Recovery

After the mission is completed, the company recovers the spacecraft and performs an nalysis to identify any issues and make improvements for future missions. This allows the rocket to be refurbished reused for future launches, reducing costs and increasing efficiency. The exact method of recovery can vary depending on the type of rocket and the company's recovery capabilities.
